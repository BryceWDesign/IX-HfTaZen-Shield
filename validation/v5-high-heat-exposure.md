# V5 High-Heat Coupon Exposure

## Stage Identity

**Stage:** V5  
**Name:** High-Heat Coupon Exposure  
**Program:** IX-HfTaZen-Shield  
**Reference configuration:** Max-01  
**Primary architecture layers under test:** HTZ-01 + OX-02 + SD-03 + TC-04, using the best surviving V4 stack direction  
**Evidence tier target if later executed:** E3-class coupon evidence  
**Execution status:** planned / frozen for v0.1  
**Purpose:** determine whether the surviving passive stack remains coherent under severe high-heat exposure consistent with the repo’s analytical thermal posture

---

## Stage Purpose

V5 exists because earlier stages can establish:

- a credible hot-face direction
- a credible buried oxidation-control direction
- a credible decoupling direction
- a credible mismatch-aware support direction

but they still do not answer the central thermal question strongly enough:

> Does the surviving passive stack remain interpretable and architecture-consistent when pushed into the severe high-heat regime it claims to care about?

V5 is the stage where the surviving passive stack stops being protected by moderate screening logic and faces a more severe coupon-level thermal challenge.

---

## What V5 Is Testing

V5 is testing the **surviving passive stack direction** under severe high-heat exposure.

The stage focuses on:

- the best surviving HTZ-01 direction from V1
- the best surviving OX-02 direction from V2
- the best surviving SD-03 / TC-04 direction from V4
- the thermal coherence of the passive stack before optional branches are allowed to claim usefulness

V5 is not yet:

- the AR-05 proof stage
- the IR-06 proof stage
- the full integrated insert stage
- the final seam-system stage

It is the severe passive-stack truth stage.

---

## Why V5 Matters

The repo has already frozen:

- Band A: **500–800 W/cm²**
- Band B: **800–1000 W/cm²**
- Band C: **1000–1500 W/cm² local only**

That means the passive stack must eventually face a stage that tests whether:

- Band A still looks credible under serious exposure
- Band B remains aggressive but not obviously fictional
- the approach to Band C exposes where passive-only logic begins to run out of honest margin

V5 exists to reveal whether the passive stack’s thermal thesis remains alive under severe heat before AR-05 is ever allowed to “save” it.

---

## V5 Test Objective

The objective of V5 is to determine whether the best surviving passive stack:

1. remains grossly coherent under severe high-heat exposure
2. behaves in a way that is broadly consistent with the analytical thermal budget
3. avoids immediate collapse of the passive-first thesis
4. reveals where the passive envelope begins to thin or break
5. produces enough truth to justify progression toward V6 damage testing and later optional-branch evaluation

---

## Primary Question

The primary V5 question is:

> Can the best surviving passive stack remain interpretable and structurally coherent under severe high-heat exposure without immediately invalidating the repo’s passive-first architecture?

---

## Secondary Questions

V5 also asks:

1. Does the surviving passive stack behave credibly in the lower and middle parts of the intended passive bands?
2. Does the stack show signs that Band B is truly aggressive rather than comfortably available?
3. Where does degradation begin to shift from bounded to architecture-threatening?
4. Does the stack fail first at the face, interface, support path, or boundary?
5. Does the observed thermal trend broadly align with the analytical budget, even if imperfectly?
6. Does the stage reveal that AR-05 might later be justified only in truly local severe hotspots rather than everywhere?

## Exposure Philosophy

The V5 exposure philosophy is:

- push the passive stack into a severe enough regime to reveal whether the architecture is honest
- do not immediately combine severe heat with every optional branch
- preserve visibility into where the stack begins to lose margin
- compare full passive logic against simpler baselines
- treat thermal, oxidation, and structural signals as linked truths

V5 is not about a single dramatic maximum number.

It is about whether the passive architecture survives scrutiny in the regime it claims to care about.

---

## Target Severity Logic

V5 should eventually be structured so the passive stack sees exposure conditions relevant to the repo’s frozen thermal posture:

- **Band A relevance:** baseline passive truth check
- **Band B relevance:** aggressive passive truth check
- **upper passive approach:** reveal where local severe burden begins to crowd passive margin too tightly

V5 is not obligated to “win” Band C as a passive stage.

In fact, one of its jobs is to show where passive-only logic starts becoming too thin.

---

## What V5 Is Trying to Prove

V5 is trying to prove only the following:

1. The passive stack remains a serious architecture under severe high-heat exposure.
2. The stack’s behavior does not immediately contradict the repo’s thermal and structural thesis.
3. The architecture still looks more coherent than simpler passive comparisons.
4. The severe-exposure results remain interpretable rather than chaotic.
5. The repo can explain where the passive envelope looks credible and where it begins to strain.

---

## What V5 Is Trying to Disprove

V5 is trying to disprove any of the following bad outcomes:

1. The passive stack collapses too early for the claimed mission posture.
2. Simpler passive comparisons perform as well or better in ways that undermine the full passive stack.
3. Severe heat reveals the stack to be face-strong but interface-weak.
4. Boundary or support-path failure dominates so strongly that the stack’s center story becomes misleading.
5. The analytical thermal budget is directionally wrong in a way that invalidates the passive posture.
6. The repo has been too optimistic about passive capability in Band B.

---

## Planned Severe-Exposure Logic

The exact exposure hardware and sequence are not frozen in v0.1, but V5 requires a severe high-heat logic capable of revealing:

- whether the passive stack remains grossly intact
- how degradation accelerates as severity increases
- how backface burden changes
- whether interface and support layers remain coherent
- whether boundary behavior diverges from center behavior
- whether thermal and structural failure cues align with or contradict the analytical budget

V5 must not be a comfortable exposure that simply repeats earlier stages at a slightly higher temperature.

It must be severe enough to test the repo’s honesty.

## Stage-Level Pass Conditions

V5 passes only if all of the following are true:

1. At least one passive stack family remains grossly coherent under severe high-heat exposure.
2. The passive stack does not immediately invalidate the repo’s thermal and structural thesis.
3. The full passive stack remains more credible than simpler passive comparisons.
4. The stage provides a clearer picture of where passive margin is comfortable, thin, or failing.
5. The results justify progression to V6 damage-state testing.

---

## Stage-Level Fail Conditions

V5 fails if any of the following occur:

1. The passive stack collapses too early to support the repo’s intended passive posture honestly.
2. Simpler passive comparisons perform as well or better in a way that undermines Max-01.
3. Severe heat reveals a dominant architecture weakness that earlier stages missed.
4. The thermal budget appears directionally unreliable in a way that requires major revision.
5. The stage cannot distinguish meaningful differences between the candidates.

If V5 fails, the passive architecture must be narrowed, simplified, or restructured before V6.

---

## V5 Keep / Cut Logic

### Keep the passive stack if:
- at least one passive family remains coherent under severe exposure
- the result still supports passive-first localized TPS logic
- the full stack earns itself relative to simpler passive comparisons

### Downgrade the passive stack if:
- severe exposure reveals that Band B or upper passive ambition was too optimistic
- the stack remains interesting but requires a narrower mission envelope
- simpler passive routes begin to look more credible in the severe regime

### Cut or restructure the current passive thesis if:
- the stack cannot remain coherent under the severe regime it claims to target
- simpler passive comparisons consistently undermine it
- the main value proposition shifts entirely toward later active rescue
