# Validation Campaign

## Module Purpose

This module defines the **v0.1 validation campaign master plan** for IX-HfTaZen-Shield.

Its job is to answer:

1. how the repo intends to move from analytical architecture toward real evidence
2. what validation stages exist and why they are ordered that way
3. what each stage is supposed to prove, disprove, or bound
4. what measurements matter at each stage
5. what conditions cause a stage to pass, fail, repeat, narrow, or cut a branch

This module does **not** claim that validation has already occurred.

It freezes the **validation structure** for v0.1.

---

## Governing Rule

IX-HfTaZen-Shield earns trust only by progressing through **staged evidence**.

The validation campaign therefore follows these rules:

- do not skip stages
- do not confuse analytical success with measured success
- do not protect weak ideas from failure
- do not combine too many variables before earlier layers earn their place
- do not let optional branches outrun the core stack
- do not expand claims faster than evidence

The validation campaign exists to force the architecture to survive contact with reality.

---

## Validation Philosophy

The campaign is built around the following principles.

### 1. Prove the smallest hard thing first
Before testing an integrated insert, prove that the material and layer logic are not obviously self-contradictory.

### 2. Failure is useful
A clean failure that explains a bad assumption is more valuable than a vague “encouraging” result.

### 3. Controls matter
A layered candidate must be compared against something simpler, not judged in a vacuum.

### 4. Boundaries matter
Seams, edges, and damaged states are not optional later concerns.

### 5. Observability matters
If a stage cannot tell what changed or why, that stage is too weak.

### 6. Optional branches must earn themselves
AR-05 and IR-06 are not baseline rights. They must be justified after the passive stack proves coherence.

---

## Validation Campaign Structure

The validation campaign is frozen into ten stages:

- V0 — Analytical Freeze
- V1 — Chemistry-Screening Coupons
- V2 — Oxidation-Control Coupons
- V3 — Thermal Shock / Cycle Coupons
- V4 — Attachment / Mismatch Stack Coupons
- V5 — High-Heat Coupon Exposure
- V6 — Deliberate Damage Coupons
- V7 — Local Active-Relief Demonstrator
- V8 — Thermal Intercept & Recovery Demonstrator
- V9 — Integrated Subscale Insert

Each stage has a defined purpose and should not be skipped.

## Stage V1 — Chemistry-Screening Coupons

## Purpose
Screen candidate hot-face directions at the material-family level before committing to deeper stack assumptions.

## Primary target
HTZ-01 candidate families

## What V1 must compare
At minimum:
- primary Hf-rich Hf–Ta–Zr–W–(C,N) lane
- reduced-complexity Hf–Ta–(C,N) lane
- simpler HfC / TaC / Ta-Hf baseline lane
- any justified reserve comparison candidate

## What V1 is trying to prove
That at least one primary-lane hot-face family remains credible enough to continue.

## What V1 is trying to disprove
- that the primary lane is needlessly complex
- that simpler lanes outperform it system-relevantly
- that the chosen direction is obviously fragile or incoherent even before stack integration

## Measurements / observations to plan
- visible cracking
- gross geometric integrity
- mass change
- surface-state change
- pre/post imagery
- comparative degradation ranking

## Controls
- simpler hot-face comparison families
- unlayered coupon comparisons

## Pass condition
At least one primary or near-primary hot-face candidate survives as a credible exposed-face direction relative to controls.

## Fail condition
The primary-lane family offers no meaningful rationale over simpler controls or shows obvious instability too early.

## If failed
- simplify HTZ-01 direction
- narrow the mission envelope
- repeat V1 before moving on

## Stage V2 — Oxidation-Control Coupons

## Purpose
Determine whether OX-02 improves the exposed-face/interfacial logic rather than merely adding brittle complexity.

## Primary target
HTZ-01 + OX-02 pair behavior

## What V2 must compare
- HTZ-01 only
- HTZ-01 + OX-02
- optionally multiple OX-02 chemistry variants

## What V2 is trying to prove
That a buried oxidation-control transition improves the stack logic relative to no-transition baselines.

## What V2 is trying to disprove
- that OX-02 is unnecessary
- that OX-02 becomes a brittle trap
- that OX-02 helps chemistry but hurts the stack overall

## Measurements / observations to plan
- visible interfacial damage
- surface and cross-sectional damage comparison
- mass change
- scale adhesion / loss indicators
- post-test sectioning or inspection logic
- thermal trend shift if instrumented

## Pass condition
The transition layer improves survivability logic or clearly reduces a relevant damage mode relative to no-transition controls.

## Fail condition
OX-02 creates more interfacial or brittle penalty than it removes.

## If failed
- change OX-02 family
- simplify the transition concept
- repeat before moving on

## Stage V3 — Thermal Shock / Cycle Coupons

## Purpose
Test whether the layered logic survives repeated thermal swings better than a rigid or oversimplified comparison stack.

## Primary target
HTZ-01 / OX-02 layered behavior under cycling, with early SD-03 logic in comparison where feasible

## What V3 is trying to prove
That the architecture is not only plausible in a single heat event, but also structurally honest under cycling stress.

## What V3 is trying to disprove
- false confidence from one-time exposures
- hidden brittleness in the layered concept
- cycling instability that defeats reuse logic early

## Measurements / observations to plan
- crack initiation
- crack growth
- edge chipping
- interfacial separation
- drift in thermal behavior between cycles
- visible deterioration trend

## Controls
- rigid-stack comparison
- no-decoupling comparison if possible

## Pass condition
The layered direction remains more coherent than rigid or oversimplified controls through repeated thermal cycles.

## Fail condition
Cycling damage rapidly destroys the key layered value proposition.

## If failed
- revise SD-03 assumptions
- revisit layer compatibility
- narrow reuse ambitions

---

## Stage V4 — Attachment / Mismatch Stack Coupons

## Purpose
Test the thermo-structural thesis directly.

## Primary target
HTZ-01 + OX-02 + SD-03 + TC-04 stack behavior under restrained or gradient-driven conditions

## What V4 is trying to prove
That SD-03 and TC-04 are doing real structural work and not just reading well on paper.

## What V4 is trying to disprove
- that hard-bond logic is equally good or better
- that the decoupling layer is too weak, too stiff, or too incoherent
- that the carrier negates the benefit of the upper stack

## Measurements / observations to plan
- crack initiation site
- displacement or strain trend if instrumented
- layer separation
- boundary distress
- thermal gradient response
- backface trend changes

## Controls
- no-decoupling stack
- alternate carrier stiffness / conductivity comparisons if feasible

## Pass condition
The decoupled stack shows a credible reduction in mismatch-driven distress versus rigid comparisons.

## Fail condition
SD-03 or TC-04 fails to earn its place.

## If failed
- redesign SD-03 / TC-04 interaction
- simplify the carrier
- cut weak variants early

## Stage V5 — High-Heat Coupon Exposure

## Purpose
Push the candidate stack into a more severe thermal regime and observe whether the analytical budget remains directionally honest.

## Primary target
HTZ-01 through TC-04 coupon-level stack under high heat exposure

## What V5 is trying to prove
That the core passive stack behaves in a way broadly consistent with the analytical budget and failure expectations.

## What V5 is trying to disprove
- that the analytical passive bands were overly generous
- that the stack fails in an unexpected dominant mode too early
- that exposed-face behavior hides deeper stack collapse

## Measurements / observations to plan
- surface temperature estimate or trend
- backface temperature
- interface-adjacent temperature if possible
- visible degradation
- mass change
- geometry change
- post-test internal inspection

## Pass condition
The stack remains interpretable and does not immediately contradict the core passive logic.

## Fail condition
The stack fails in a way that collapses the passive-first thesis within the intended target band.

## If failed
- narrow the passive bands
- revise hot-face choice
- revise transition or carrier logic
- repeat with corrected assumptions

## Stage V6 — Deliberate Damage Coupons

## Purpose
Break the fantasy of perfect hardware early.

## Primary target
Damaged-state behavior of the passive stack and joint-adjacent regions

## Required damage classes to plan
- chipped edge
- corner loss
- surface flaw
- seam or gap perturbation
- localized boundary imperfection

## What V6 is trying to prove
That the architecture fails in a bounded, inspectable way rather than catastrophically and invisibly from minor damage.

## What V6 is trying to disprove
- perfection dependence
- hidden fragility at edges and seams
- unrealistic tolerance assumptions

## Measurements / observations to plan
- damage-growth pattern
- local thermal shift
- perimeter versus center response
- post-test internal damage mapping
- change in backface trend relative to undamaged controls

## Pass condition
Damage produces bounded, interpretable degradation rather than immediate architecture invalidation.

## Fail condition
Minor damage collapses the insert too easily for the intended mission posture.

## If failed
- increase SJ-08 emphasis
- revise segmentation logic
- narrow reuse ambition or use-case boundary

## Stage V7 — Local Active-Relief Demonstrator

## Purpose
Prove whether AR-05 is a real thermal helper or just complexity theater.

## Primary target
A local-only severe-hotspot relief concept

## Allowed branch types
- porous-UHTC transpiration-style relief
- refractory heat-pipe local intercept
- other tightly bounded local-relief concept if justified

## What V7 is trying to prove
That a bounded local branch can reduce severe hotspot burden without causing worse downstream or structural penalty.

## What V7 is trying to disprove
- vague “advanced cooling” logic
- active relief that merely moves the failure
- active branches that create worse oxidation or interface paths

## Measurements / observations to plan
- local burden reduction
- downstream thermal change
- structural penalty
- integration penalty
- any new damage concentration near the active branch

## Pass condition
AR-05 reduces local burden measurably and remains bounded.

## Fail condition
AR-05 creates worse side effects than the hotspot it was meant to solve.

## If failed
- cut AR-05 from Max-01
- keep the repo passive-first
- document why the branch lost

## Validation Order Rules

The order of stages is fixed by logic.

### Rule V-1
Do not enter V2 until V1 identifies at least one credible hot-face direction.

### Rule V-2
Do not enter V4 until V2 and V3 show the stack is at least worth integrating further.

### Rule V-3
Do not elevate AR-05 until the passive stack has earned V5-level attention.

### Rule V-4
Do not elevate IR-06 until AR-05 or another controlled intercept source is clearly justified.

### Rule V-5
Do not call V9 meaningful if boundaries, damage states, and observability were never addressed earlier.

---

## Controls and Comparators

Every serious stage should include controls where practical.

### Minimum control philosophy
- compare layered against simpler baselines
- compare decoupled against rigid baselines
- compare damaged against undamaged
- compare with optional branch versus without optional branch
- compare perimeter effects against center effects where possible

Without controls, the campaign risks measuring activity instead of progress.

---

## Measurement Minimums Across the Campaign

The campaign should preserve visibility into the following wherever relevant:

- visible surface state
- mass change
- geometry change
- backface temperature
- interface-adjacent thermal trend where possible
- crack / spall / edge damage
- seam or boundary behavior
- strain or displacement trend where possible
- before/after comparison artifacts
- model-versus-measurement disagreement

These are the minimum truths the campaign wants from later evidence.

---

## Pass / Fail Discipline

A stage is not successful because it produces interesting data.

A stage is successful only if it answers the question it was meant to answer.

Examples:
- a V1 result is not a V5 result
- a V5 coupon is not a V9 integrated insert
- a recovery demo is not proof of TPS maturity
- a single pretty sample does not overrule a damage-state failure

The campaign must stay ruthless about this.

---

## Branch Keep / Cut Rules

### AR-05 keep rule
AR-05 stays only if V7 shows it reduces local burden without creating worse total architecture penalty.

### AR-05 cut rule
If V7 reveals oxidation, structural, or integration harm greater than the benefit, AR-05 is cut.

### IR-06 keep rule
IR-06 stays only if V8 shows secondary value without harming the stack.

### IR-06 cut rule
If IR-06 creates leak-seeking behavior, weakens the thermal choke, or adds unjustified complexity, it is cut.

### Max-01 keep rule
Max-01 stays the reference only if V0 through V9 continue to describe the same basic architecture truthfully.

### Max-01 revision rule
If integrated evidence forces a major conceptual change, the repo must branch into a new reference configuration rather than quietly rewriting history.

## Evidence Escalation Path

The validation campaign maps roughly to the repo’s evidence ladder:

- **V0** supports E0 / E1 discipline
- **V1–V6** move into E3-class coupon evidence when eventually executed

This progression matters because the repo must not claim higher evidence tiers before the corresponding validation stage exists.

- freeze the concept
- screen the hot-face direction
- test oxidation-control logic

- test mismatch logic
- test severe heat
